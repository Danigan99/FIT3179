The Declining Success of Southpaw Fighters

An opportune trend for gamblers is the declining success of Southpaw fighters. This refers to fighters who place their right foot and hand in front (in the slightly side-on stance that fighters maintain) so that the right hand is used for jabs, and the left limbs are used for power shots. As the dominant style for left-handed fighters, a key advantage of this was the less familiarity that Orthodox fighters (who put the opposite side foward) would have against such a stance. However, as time has eroded its novelty, the Southpaw stance is increasingly succumbing to structural weaknesses such as having their lead hand further from their opponent's liver.

Southpaws have steadily Lost more Fights since 2015

Grapplers often make Safer Bets against Strikers

Grapplers have long dominated the top of UFC rankings. This refers to fighters whose primary style involves winning fights on the ground where neither fighter is standing upright. Thus, entailing wrestlers and specialists in submissions (forcing the opponent to tap). While Strikers (fighters who specialise in attacks on their feet) have the advantage of having every round start upright, the best grapplers are usually sufficiently capable of getting close enough to take their opponents down. At this point, effective strikes are neutralised, and Grapplers can then hold their opponents down to win a decision or finish their opponents. Even when considering all fighters, its important to note that the average difference in knockouts (KO) between the victorious and losing fighters, is more variable than with submissions (SUB), because stand-up fights leave fewer avenues for keeping your opponent's threats neutralised while you advance. In other words, you are more likely to get knocked out by a lucky punch, than a lucky submission.
